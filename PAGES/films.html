When it comes to films, I find myself drawn to stories that really dig into what it means to be human, all those messy, beautiful complexities. Everything Everywhere All at Once with its wild ride through multiverses, Isle of Dogs with its quirky charm and heart, and Dead Poets Society, that classic ode to living passionately – they all resonate deeply. Then, when I need to unwind, it's all about lighthearted fun. Brooklyn Nine-Nine delivers those consistent laughs, The Amazing World of Gumball is just pure, unadulterated silliness, and Strong Woman: Do Bong Soon offers a cozy, relaxing escape with a hint of kilig. It's a nice balance, you know? A little bit of soul-searching and a whole lot of good vibes.
